The Origin of Origami


Origami is the most recreational art of Japanese for centuries. They made it as a part of their culture to foster the creativity among youngsters. Origami is served as an elegant yet amusing activity of Japanese done during their leisure times.


Example of Peace through Origami


When a 12-year old girl Sadako Sasaki got bedridden and suffered from Leukemia which was the result of the atomic bomb on Hiroshima, she decided to make 1000 Origami cranes and spread the message of peace around the world. Though she could make 644 only, her friends and relatves completed her aim and this incident created ripples in their society. Her statue at Hroshima peace park and the making of Origami have tangles the hearts of Japanese people creating a deep respect towards this art of Origami making as symbol of life and peace
